Discover Tirana, Albania

Welcome to Tirana, the vibrant capital of Albania! Explore its colorful buildings, rich history, and lively culture.

Tirana was officially established in 1614. As you explore the city, you’ll encounter a striking mix of architectural styles, from Ottoman mosques and Fascist-era government buildings to Soviet-style blocks and bold, modern art installations. This eclectic landscape reflects Albania’s complex past and its forward-looking spirit.

Plan your visit to experience the unique blend of old traditions and modern life in Tirana.

Some must-see attractions include the Skanderbeg Square and the National History Museum.

The population of Tirana is approximately 535,7021, and its metro area covers 464 km2.

Top Activities in Tirana

  • Visit the famous Skanderbeg Square in downtown Tirana
  • Learn about the country's history at the Albanian History Museum
  • Enjoy local Albanian cuisine, and try some of the country's national dishes, like byrek

Travel Tips

  1. Learn some basic Albanian phrases
  2. Become comfortable with using Albanian currency called Lek
  3. Respect local customs and traditions

Skanderbeg Square

Albanian National History Museum

The image shows the grand entrance to the Albanian National History Museum in Tirana, located on Skanderbeg Square. The building is modernist in design with a wide staircase leading to tall white columns and large glass doors. Above the entrance is a striking and iconic mosaic called "The Albanians," depicting figures from various periods of Albania’s history, from ancient Illyrians to Communist-era partisans. The square in front is often filled with tourists and locals enjoying the public space.
